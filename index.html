Air Pollution in New Zealand

New Zealand's air pollution is relatively low, but still a cause for concern.
High levels of agriculture contribute to air pollution, due to:

Synthetic fertilisers. These can release ammonia and nitrous oxide into the air.

Poor management of animal waste. Manure can further contribute to ammonia released into the air.

Tilling of soil releases dust into the air, which counts as particulate matter.

Further contributing to air pollution is our widespread use of woodburners to heat homes, along with emissions from transport and industry.
