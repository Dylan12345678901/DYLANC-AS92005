What are the statistics associated with pollution in New Zealand?

New Zealand has an unusual global emissions profile when compared to the rest of the world.
For example, 48% of emissions came from the agriculture industry, compared to only 24% global average, and only 11% average for
Kyoto Protocol Annex 1 countries. This is due to our economic reliance on exporting animal products such as dairy, wool, and meat.

Air pollution is increasing every year, often faster than it ever has before. In 2023, the average PM10 (microparticles in the air) concentration in Auckland increased by 1.8%
compared to 2022. In addition to this, concentration of PM2.5 (smaller microscopic particles) increased by 1.6% in 2023 compared to 2022.
